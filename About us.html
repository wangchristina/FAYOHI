Management Team

Dr. Ahmed Sarki

Founder and Executive Director

Dr. Sarki holds a Ph.D. in Medicine (Epidemiology and Biostatistics), University of Warwick, UK; MSc Public Health (with Distinction) from Oxford Brookes University, UK; BSc Microbiology from Bayero University, Kano, Nigeria; and Diploma in Information Systems Management (with Distinction) from APTECH Computer Education.

Dr. Sarki also holds a Certificate in Leadership and Management in Health from the University of Washington; Certificate in ‘Project Management’ from HiiT (Abuja Center); Certificate in ‘Monitoring and Evaluation’ from E&Management Consult, sponsored by the American Centers for Disease Control (CDC) (Nigeria office) through African Field Epidemiology Network (AFENET). Dr. Sarki is currently a Senior Faculty at the School of Nursing and Midwifery, Faculty of Health Sciences, Aga Khan University, Uganda. He was a postdoctoral researcher at Oxford Brookes University, UK, and a postdoctoral research consultant for Population Council Kenya. Dr. Sarki was a research associate on a maternal health project in Northern Nigeria. Furthermore, Dr. Sarki was the Coordinator for Sub-Saharan Africa Research Network at the Institute of Advanced Study, University of Warwick, UK. He was a volunteer at Society for Family Health (SFH) Nigeria during the large-scale public education of Affordable Medicines For Malaria (AMFm) project sponsored by Global Fund and Roll Back Malaria in the year 2011. Dr. Sarki has several years of experience in designing and implementing public health intervention projects in low resource settings.

Dr. Chidozie Nduka

Head of Research and Development

Dr. Nduka holds a medical degree (MBBS) from the College of Medicine, University of Nigeria Nsukka, he practiced as a physician in the general areas of medicine and in primary healthcare in Northern and Southern Nigeria respectively. Beyond his experience in Nigeria, Dr. Nduka briefly worked in sub-urban Kenya to improve the lives of vulnerable groups through clinical medicine and research.

Dr. Nduka obtained an MPH (with distinction) and a Ph.D. in Health Sciences from Warwick Medical School, University of Warwick, UK, and thereafter worked as a Senior Research Fellow in Clinical Effectiveness at the same University. Dr. Nduka has worked as a consultant on CDC/USAID funded projects with the Centre for Integrated Health Programs (CIHP)- a non-governmental organization with focus in HIV/AIDS programming in Nigeria.

Maryam Ahmad Abubakar

Secretary and Legal Officer

Maryam holds an LLB from Bayero University Kano, Nigeria. She has over 3 years of experience working with prominent organisations across Nigeria which include Planned Parenthood Federation of Nigeria/Jameel Poverty Action Lab, World Health Organisation Kano Office, and Continental Research of Nigeria.

Maryam is an active memeber of several NGOs which include: KAYCAD, Creative Business Forum, Child Protection Initiative, Women Support Group, and Centre for Young Peoples Advancement and Community development.

Hajiya Kubra Abdulsalam

Head of Planning and Implementation

Hajiya Kubra holds a HND in Agric Home Economics from Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria. She is an experienced extension worker with more than 25 years’ worth of experience. She has also worked with WHO, UNICEF, DfiD on several health intervention projects. She also sits on the board of several NGOs.

Bashir Mohammed

Head of Procurement

Bashir holds an OND Architecture from Federal Polytechnic Bauchi, and a BSc Architecture from Kano State University of Science and Technology, Wudil. Bashir has more than seven years’ experience in general procurement.

Isah Musa Auyo

Management Information System (MIS) Officer

Isa holds a Postgraduate Diploma in Information Management, mB.A. Library and Information Science, and Diploma in Computer Science all from Bayero University, Kano, Nigeria. Isah has more than five years’ experience in MIS in the non-profit industry.

Our drive is to empower Nigerians by the year 2030 through information, and effective interventions to lead healthier lives irrespective of age, gender, ethnicity and place of residence (rural or urban) through our work.
