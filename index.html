Family and Youth Health Initiative (FAYOHI) is a non-profit, non-governmental organization established in April 2012 to become part of the campaign for reducing preventable diseases and deaths in Nigeria, especially areas that have not been impacted by on-going health programs in the country.

The first target state of the initiative is Jigawa state, Northwestern Nigeria. The choice of the target state arose after careful analyses and consideration of the health disparities existing in the country as well as the activities of various governmental and non-governmental agencies across the country. Overtime, we hope to open offices in other states that are struggling from wide health inequalities.


School-based Health Promotion

in Jigawa and Kano States to identify the health needs of adolescents and develop improvements to healthcare practice and policy in the region

NCD Screening

aims to determine the prevalence of hypertension, diabetes, high blood lipids, and obesity among rural populations in Jigawa state.

End Maternal Mortality

tackling postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) with EMM by empowering local communities, organizations and initiatives to help distributed proven maternal health solutions.

Our drive is to empower Nigerians by the year 2030 through information, and effective interventions to lead healthier lives irrespective of age, gender, ethnicity and place of residence (rural or urban) through our work.
